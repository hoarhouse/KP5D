How to Search Across All Your AI Conversations (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)

Chris Hoar • February 2026 • 8 min read

I spent 20 minutes yesterday searching for a database indexing conversation I knew I'd had recently. Checked ChatGPT history. Nothing. Tried Claude's search. Nope. Finally found it in Gemini, filed under a completely different title than I remembered.

This isn't a workflow problem. It's a structural one.

Every AI platform builds its own conversation silo. You can search within ChatGPT, within Claude, within Gemini. But you can't search across them. And with each new model release, each new platform launch, the fragmentation gets worse.

What each platform offers today

ChatGPT

Basic title search. Filters by date. Can star conversations. No content search, no tags, no export API.

Claude

Title and content search. Projects for organization. Better than ChatGPT but still locked to Claude-only conversations.

Gemini

Google-powered search across your conversations. Good for finding specific chats. Zero interoperability with other platforms.

Each platform's search works fine in isolation. But isolation is the problem.

Why platforms will never solve this

OpenAI isn't going to build Claude conversation import. Anthropic won't index your ChatGPT history. Google certainly won't aggregate competitor data.

It's the same reason Gmail doesn't search your Outlook emails. Competitive lock-in is a feature, not a bug, of platform economics.

And the gap keeps widening. DeepSeek launches. Grok goes mainstream. Perplexity adds conversation history. Every new model means another silo, another place your knowledge disappears into.

The platforms have zero incentive to fix this. The solution has to come from outside.

The actual cost of this fragmentation

Decisions disappear. That perfect problem-solving approach you worked out with ChatGPT? Lost in a sea of "New conversation" titles.

Context resets constantly. You rebuild the same context with Claude that you already built with ChatGPT last week. Hours of redundant work.

Patterns stay invisible. You can't see that you've asked variations of the same question across three platforms over six months. No learning compounds.

We're building incredible conversations with AI, then letting them evaporate.

Current workarounds and why they fall short

Manual exports. ChatGPT lets you download your data every few weeks. Claude has conversation export. But these are snapshots, not systems. By the time you export, you've already lost the context you needed.

Browser extensions. ChatGPT Exporter and similar tools work conversation by conversation. Fine for archiving specific chats. Useless for ongoing search needs.

Echoes by R2bits. Actually solid for search. Their Chrome extension captures conversations, indexes them, makes them searchable. 850+ people in their Discord proves the need. But it's pure retrieval, no knowledge extraction. You can find conversations but not patterns.

Copy-paste to Obsidian/Notion. The manual factor kills this. The moment you have to remember to copy something, you've already lost. The conversations you forget to save are always the ones you need.

All these approaches treat symptoms. None address the core issue: conversations need to be captured automatically, stored locally, searchable instantly, and intelligent about extraction.

Enter Kept: Open source, local-first, automatic

We're building what the platforms won't. A desktop app, Chrome extension, and CLI that archives conversations from ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini automatically, indexes them locally with SQLite FTS5, and gives you four core tools:

  • Chat Archival to download and manage conversations in one place
  • Conversation Forks to continue any chat in a different model
  • Context Templating to keep your project files synced with LLM sessions through the CLI
  • Dynamic Links to connect information across separate chats into a knowledge graph

The bigger picture

Multi-LLM workflows are accelerating. You use ChatGPT for creative work. Claude for code. Gemini for research. Specialized models for specific tasks.

This is good. Competition drives innovation. Model diversity prevents lock-in.

But without unified search, without pattern recognition across platforms, we're trading capability for chaos. Every new model makes us potentially smarter but practically more fragmented.

The platforms won't fix this. They can't. Their incentives run the opposite direction.

The solution has to be local, open, and platform-agnostic. It has to treat your conversations as a unified knowledge base, not scattered fragments.

Frequently asked questions

Can I search across ChatGPT and Claude at the same time?
Not natively. ChatGPT and Claude each have their own search functions, but they don't connect. You need a third-party tool like Kept or manual export methods to search across both platforms.
Why won't AI platforms add cross-platform search?
Competitive lock-in. OpenAI won't index Anthropic's conversations and vice versa. It's the same reason Gmail doesn't search Outlook. The solution has to come from outside the platforms.
What's the best way to export AI conversations?
For automatic capture: Kept (open source) or Echoes. For manual exports: ChatGPT's data export feature or browser extensions like ChatGPT Exporter. The key is consistency, which is why automatic tools work better long-term.
Is my conversation data safe with third-party tools?
Depends on the tool. Kept stores everything locally in SQLite on your machine. Echoes and others may use cloud storage. Always check where your data lives and who has access.
Can I build my own AI conversation search tool?
Yes. You can export conversations via API or browser automation, store in a database, and use full-text search. But maintaining parsers for multiple platforms is complex. That's why tools like Kept exist.
