How to Transfer Context Between ChatGPT and Claude (Without Starting Over)

Chris Hoar • February 2026 • 7 min read

Every developer who uses multiple AI tools hits the same wall. You are 30 messages deep in a ChatGPT conversation about your application architecture. You have established the tech stack, discussed trade-offs, made decisions. Now you want Claude's perspective on a specific part.

But Claude knows nothing about any of it.

You are back to zero. The context you spent an hour building is locked inside one platform.

The manual methods (and their costs)

Ask ChatGPT to summarize the conversation, copy summary, paste into Claude. Works but loses nuance. Decisions get flattened into bullet points, and you lose the reasoning trail.

Export conversation as Markdown, upload to Claude Project. Better fidelity but multiple steps, breaks your flow, and Projects have upload limits.

Use "Retry in Another AI" type extensions that copy the thread. Convenient but copies everything including irrelevant tangents. No intelligence about what context actually matters.

The real problem: at low frequency (once a month) any of these work fine. But power users switch platforms multiple times per day. ChatGPT for code. Claude for architecture. Gemini for research. DeepSeek for a different perspective.

At that frequency, manual transfer is a productivity tax you pay every single day.

Why this problem exists

Each AI platform is a walled garden by design. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are competing for the same users. They have zero incentive to make it easy to leave mid-conversation.

This is not a technical limitation. The APIs exist. The data formats are compatible. It is a business decision.

Every platform wants to be the only platform you use. Context lock-in is a feature, not a bug, from their perspective.

The user is the one who pays the cost.

What context portability actually means

It is not just copying text. Real context portability means understanding which parts of a conversation are relevant to the new task, preserving the decision history and reasoning, carrying over project-specific terminology and constraints, and packaging it in a format the receiving platform can actually use effectively.

A 30-message conversation might contain 3 messages worth of transferable context. The rest is back-and-forth, corrections, and tangents.

Intelligent context transfer means extracting signal from noise.

Beyond platform-to-platform: the repo context problem

There is a related problem that compounds this. Every time you open a new AI conversation, you re-explain your codebase. Here is my tech stack. Here are the main files. Here is the architecture. This is the convention we use.

Developers do this dozens of times per week across different platforms. The setup overhead is constant.

If your conversation context and your repository context were both available locally and packaged automatically, you could start any AI conversation on any platform with full background already loaded. No re-explaining. No setup ritual.

Kept approaches this differently

The multi-model workflow is not going away

Developers are not going to consolidate to one AI tool. The models have different strengths and those strengths shift with every release cycle.

The workflow of the future is not picking the best model. It is using the right model for each task and having your context follow you.

The tools that enable this portability will become infrastructure, not optional plugins.

The platforms will not build it because they are competitors. It has to come from outside.

Frequently asked questions

Can you transfer a conversation from ChatGPT to Claude?
Not natively. The platforms don't talk to each other. Current options are manual (summarize and paste, export markdown, use extensions like Retry in Another AI). Kept automates this with Conversation Forks, which packages context and lets you start a new session in any platform with background loaded.
Does Claude have access to my ChatGPT conversations?
No. Claude and ChatGPT are built by competing companies (Anthropic and OpenAI). They have no data sharing. Each platform only knows about conversations that happened within it.
What is AI context portability?
The ability to move your conversation context, project background, and decisions from one AI platform to another without manually re-explaining everything. Currently no platform supports this natively because they are competitors.
How do developers use multiple AI models effectively?
Most developers use different models for different strengths. ChatGPT for code generation, Claude for architecture and reasoning, Gemini for research with Google integration. The challenge is maintaining context across them. Kept's Context Templating and CLI help by keeping your project files synced with every LLM session automatically.
