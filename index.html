The structural advantage

They'll never build this for each other.

ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are all adding memory and search — inside their own walls. But they're competing companies. They will never index each other's conversations. That gap is permanent.

Gmail doesn't show your Outlook emails. That's why email clients like Spark and Superhuman exist. The same structural gap exists for AI platforms — and it widens as more models launch.

Kept is the unified intelligence layer that sits above all of them. One knowledge graph. One search. Context that moves between them. Every platform.

The problem

Fragmented context. Lost conversations. Wasted time.

You work across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and other providers. You split context across dozens of separate chats. But there is no way to centralize those conversations or organize the information locally.

Meanwhile, transferring context between your projects and LLM providers means tedious copy-pasting through the browser. Formatting breaks, critical information gets left out, and the files you need rarely change. The workflow tax is real.

How it works

Four steps from conversation to knowledge

01

Capture

The Kept extension detects your AI conversations across providers and archives them locally. No copy-paste, no manual exports. Also supports bulk import from platform data exports for your full history.

02

Index

Desktop client writes conversations to a local vault with full metadata. SQLite FTS5 delivers instant full-text search across thousands of conversations from every provider.

03

Extract

Dynamic Links connect information across separate chats. Build a knowledge graph of relationships between conversations, projects, and ideas regardless of which provider they came from.

04

Work

Fork a conversation to continue it in a different model. Define context templates that keep your project files synced with your LLM sessions. The CLI monitors your codebase so the agent always sees current state.
